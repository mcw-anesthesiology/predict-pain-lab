Collaborative Health Outcomes Information Registry (CHOIR) Electronic patient reported outcomes clinical research data registry

We are establishing a clinical research warehouse bank to collect and store electronically obtained patient reported outcomes (ePROs) using the Collaborative Health Outcomes Information Registry (CHOIR) platform from Froedtert Health, Children's Hospital of Wisconsin, and MCW patients in a manner conducive to increasing the potential for campus-based initiatives to maintain competitiveness with similar institutions. The clinical records will be updated regularly with new information and changes inserted into the bank (CHOIR Registry).

This CHOIR Registry will organize the data from a research perspective with plans to utilize this collection and structure for future unspecified research projects. The primary benefit of this CHOIR Registry is to bring together ePRO clinical data from disparate clinical sources (including FH clinics, MCW Clinics, CHW clinics) into a single ePRO repository and stored from the research perspective.

Identifying opioid response phenotypes in low back pain electronic health data

Low back pain is the leading cause of disability worldwide. Yet, despite this significant health burden, we lack predictive models for clinical care. Unfortunately, clinicians lack clear guidance for treatment, specifically regarding the use of opioids.

This proposal would accelerate pain medicine research by translating clinical experiences into discrete and analyzable data, specifically examining opioid response patterns for patients with low back pain in the electronic health record. Ultimately, this would provide a foundation for advancing clinical care as well as future approaches to genomic and personalized medicine.

Delivering ABA pain content to the millennial learner Creation and validation of an anesthesiology pain educational app

The goal of this project is to create an educational modality that allows anesthesiology trainees to obtain core pain knowledge via a learner friendly platform, implementable nationwide at residency programs. We will assess whether using an App to transform core pain-domain knowledge into smaller elements and solidifying mastery of this material using testing with novel gamification techniques will improve engagement, learning, and performance.

An integrative and sustainable approach to pain management in primary care

The goal of this research is to determine effectiveness of clinician pain education and an electronic patient report outcomes tool (CHOIR) in primary care practices on patient satisfaction and pain relief.

Advancing pain education for interdisciplinary health learners in SE Wisconsin

This research will develop and implement an electronic educational platform focused on non-opioid alternative to the care of patients with opioids.

Designing user-centered decision support tools for chronic pain in primary care

This research will improve understanding of how primary care clinicians access and use patient information to provide the best possible care for patients with chronic pain. With this knowledge, this study will develop new information technology tools that help physicians make evidence-based care decisions and improve the lives of the millions of Americans who suffer from chronic pain.

Opioid risk tool Implementation evaluation and extension of validity and reliability

This research examines the validity and reliability of tools utilized in primary and specialized care to predict opioid misuse, abuse, and diversion of controlled medications. With this knowledge, we can improve patient pain outcomes along with the patient and community safety.

This study will further prompt new technology information tools that help physicians make evidence-based care decisions and improve the safety of prescribing controlled medications.
